Digital Forensics

Digital forensics represents a special field within forensic science dedicated to identifying, acquiring, analyzing, and presenting electronic data. Because electronic evidence is significant in almost every type of criminal activity, digital forensics is required to support investigations by law enforcement officials...

Digital forensics exists primarily to retrieve and scrutinize digital data, thereby producing analyses that can be used as evidence during legal proceedings. Each technique employed during digital forensics is based on dependable forensic practices to make certain the evidence can be used legally. (INTERPOL, n.d.)
